About Me

Hi! I'm a first-year PhD student at the department of Germanic Studies at the University of Texas at Austin.

I have an MA in Computational Psycholinguistics from the University of Antwerp, where I worked under the supervision of professor Sarah Bernolet and professor Orphée De Clercq from Ghent University on the automatic assessment of the syntactic complexity of texts written by learners of Dutch as a second language. Before that, I studied German, English and Spanish Linguistics and Literature at Ghent University.

My favorite acronyms are SLA – Second Language Acquisition, and NLP – Natural Language Processing. I'm interested in Language Pedagogy and the application of SLA theories in the development of Computer-Assisted Language Learning applications, the psycholinguistic study of SLA and its analysis with the use of computational tools, automatic proficiency and readability assessment and the linguistic representations, AI for accessible education and language learning, and the possible insights on language that we can get from modern language models.

I have more hobbies than time allows, but among them I count running, (trying to) longboard, reading, watercolor painting and improving my coding skills. I also spend a significant amount of time with my cat Luke and my ferret Chewie.
