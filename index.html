About Me

Hi! I'm a first-year PhD student at the department of Germanic Studies at the University of Texas at Austin.

I have an MA in Computational Psycholinguistics from the University of Antwerp, where I worked under the supervision of professor Sarah Bernolet and professor Orphée Declercq from Ghent University. Before that, I studied German, English and Spanish Linguistics and Literature at Ghent University.

My favorite acronyms are SLA – Second Language Acquisition, and NLP – Natural Language Processing. I'm interested in Language Pedagogy and the application of SLA theories in the development of Computer-Assisted Language Learning tools for German, the psycholinguistic study of SLA and its analysis with the use of computational tools, and automatic proficiency and readability assessement.

I have more hobbies than time allows, but among them I count running, (trying to) longboard, reading and watercolor painting. I also dedicate a significant amount of time with my cat Luke.
